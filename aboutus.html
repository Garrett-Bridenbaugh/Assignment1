About the Developer

Garrett Bridenbaugh

Associate Director of Program Management and Program Director at the USF Institute of Applied Engineering

Garrett Bridenbaugh serves as both the Associate Director of Program Management and Program Director of the Academic Consortium at the University of South Florida’s Institute of Applied Engineering, a 501(c)(3) organization dedicated to advancing impactful solutions for our nation’s defense challenges. His broad background in engineering, military leadership, and business management provides a strong foundation for his work in both roles.

Bridenbaugh began his career as a structural engineer at General Dynamics Electric Boat, contributing to the design of Columbia Class nuclear submarines. Following this, he served as an officer in the U.S. Army’s 101st Airborne Division, where he earned both Ranger and Sapper tabs. His time in the Army instilled in him a commitment to teamwork and resilience.

As Associate Director, he leads a team managing a diverse portfolio of defense and advanced technology projects. As Program Director, he has grown the consortium to include over 40 universities, strengthening the bridge between academia and national defense.

He holds a Bachelor of Science in Mechanical Engineering from Florida Institute of Technology, an Executive MBA from the University of South Florida, and is currently pursuing a Master of Science in Artificial Intelligence and Business Analytics.

About This Project

Project Overview

This web application explores U.S. housing data through interactive charts and visual insights — helping users compare their listings, observe market trends, and analyze price behaviors regionally. The design emphasizes usability, responsiveness, and informative visuals for analysts, homeowners, and developers.

Technologies Used

  • HTML5 & CSS3
  • Bootstrap 5 for layout and responsiveness
  • JavaScript & Chart.js for data visualizations
  • Visual Studio Code (VS Code)

My Experience Making This Site

Creating this site was a great way to apply what I’ve been learning in web development. I wanted it to feel clean and easy to use while showing off real estate data in a meaningful way. Getting the charts and layout to work across different screen sizes took some trial and error.

I used Bootstrap for responsiveness, Chart.js for visualizations, and VS Code to keep everything organized. Working with real data made things more interesting—and more challenging—but it helped me see how design and functionality come together in real-world projects.

I faced several challenges implementing the CRUD functionality, especially when trying to use user-defined inputs without direct access to the full dataset. Due to limitations in referencing live data, I had to hard-code some assumptions. In a real-world scenario, I would have connected to a live database using SQL to dynamically query the dataset and enable more robust and scalable data analysis.
